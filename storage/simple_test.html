Frequently Asked Questions

To get a scholarship in Malaysia, students must generally meet one or more of the following requirements:

  • Academic threshold: typically from 1A onwards for STPM/A-Level or 5As onwards for SPM/IGCSE. University-sponsored scholarships have much lower requirements for fee waivers.
  • Non-academic achievements: demonstrate talent, leadership or active involvement in co-curricular endeavours like being a state sports player or president of a student organisation.
  • Disadvantaged background: Deserving students from specific household income level (B40/M40), with single parent, or with disabilities.

For a personalised list of scholarships matched specifically to your academic results and profile, you can use the scholarship matching feature on the Uni Enrol website.

The number of A's required depends entirely on the prestige and type of the scholarship:

  • Top-Tier Government & Corporate (e.g., JPA Scholarship, Petronas Sponsorship): Typically require straight A+ or a minimum of 8A+/9A+ in SPM.

  • Private University Waivers: Full tuition fee waivers often require straight As (SPM A+, A, A-). However, partial scholarships are frequently available for students with 3As to 5As (SPM/IGCSE) or even 1A (A-Level/STPM/UEC), and bursaries may be offered to students who meet the course entry requirements.

The application process of external scholarships follows a standard cycle, with most providers opening the application immediately after results release of major Malaysian exams like SPM or STPM. Follow these steps to maximise your success rate:

-Research & Shortlist: Identify if you are eligible for Government (JPA/MARA), Corporate, or University awards.

  • Prepare Documentation: Gather certified copies of your SPM/STPM results, parents' income slips (EA Form), and co-curricular certificates.
  • Submit Online: Most applications, including JPA and Petronas, are submitted via their respective online portals.
  • Attend Assessment: Shortlisted candidates for high-value scholarships often undergo an interview or a camp assessment.

Tip: On Uni Enrol, you can browse a comprehensive list of scholarships for Malaysians and see requirements, award amounts, and terms in one place.

Securing scholarships for funding to study overseas is highly competitive.

  • Government Routes: The JPA/MARA scholarship is the primary route for top SPM leavers to study in the UK, US, or Australia. MARA also offers postgraduate funding Malaysia and loans for specific fields abroad.
  • Corporate Sponsorship: Major corporations like Petronas, Bank Negara, and Khazanah Nasional offer full scholarships for students to study at top global universities, often bonded with an employment contract upon graduation.
  • University-Specific: Many foreign universities offer partial scholarships for international students, including Malaysian students.
